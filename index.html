What is Ihsan?

In Islamic terminology, **Ihsan ($\text{ÿ•ÿ≠ÿ≥ÿßŸÜ}$)** is often translated as "perfection," "excellence," or "doing good." It represents the highest level of faith, where a believer not only adheres to the rules (Islam) and believes in the core tenants (Iman) but also performs all actions as if they see God, or at least knowing that God sees them. This constant awareness leads to acts of ultimate kindness and sincerity.

"Ihsan is to worship Allah as if you see Him, for if you do not see Him, He surely sees you." (Hadith of Gabriel)

Rahma ($\text{ÿ±ÿ≠ŸÖÿ©}$ - Mercy)

*Rahma* is the attribute of universal compassion and mercy, deeply rooted in the Quran. The term is the foundation of every chapter (except one) of the Quran, starting with "Bismillah ir-Rahman ir-Rahim" (In the Name of God, the Most Compassionate, the Most Merciful). It dictates that kindness must extend beyond believers to all of humanity and the environment.

Adl ($\text{ÿπÿØŸÑ}$ - Justice)

True kindness cannot exist without justice. *Adl* means treating every person fairly, regardless of their status, wealth, or belief. The concept of kindness is interwoven with establishing equilibrium and rights, ensuring that charity (an act of kindness) complements legal justice.

Kindness to Family and Kinship

The first recipients of kindness are one's own parents and family. This includes honoring them, speaking to them gently, and providing for them. Islam places great emphasis on maintaining ties of kinship (*Silat ar-Rahim*), which is considered a hallmark of true faith.

Charity and Generosity (Sadaqah)

Kindness is manifested through charitable giving (*Sadaqah*), which can be financial or non-financial (like a smile, removing a harmful object from a path, or offering guidance). This ensures the welfare of the broader community and reduces social inequality.

Compassion to Animals and the Earth

Kindness extends to all living beings. The Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) taught that treating animals well is a path to paradise, while cruelty is a cause for punishment. Believers are entrusted with the stewardship (*Khilafah*) of the Earth, requiring responsible use of resources and environmental protection.

The Result of Ihsan

Living by the standard of Ihsan transforms daily life into an act of worship. It fosters a peaceful society where individuals strive for moral perfection, leading to internal tranquility and communal harmony.

Strive for excellence in all that you do.
